Oxfordshire Data Insight System

Overview

 The Oxfordshire Data Insight System is a comprehensive tool developed to provide valuable insights into house prices, broadband speeds, and council tax charges across Oxfordshire. The system integrates data from various sources to support residents, prospective movers, and decision-makers with accurate and actionable information.

Objectives

1. House Prices: Analyze and compare property values across different wards and districts. 

2. Broadband Speed: Assess broadband availability and speed across Oxfordshire.

3. Council Tax Charges: Review and compare council tax charges for different property bands in various districts.

Data Collection and Cleaning

Data Sources:

 House Prices: Office for National Statistics (ONS) ONS DatasetBroadband Speed: House of Commons Library Broadband DatasetCouncil Tax: Oxfordshire District Councils Council Tax Dataset

Cleaning Process:

Used Excel to remove unnecessary rows and update column names for clarity.Utilized R programming for advanced data cleaning, including handling missing values and eliminating duplicates.

Data Model and Implementation

Normalization and Schema Design:

Implemented third normal form (3NF) to eliminate redundancy and optimize database structure. Created tables for DISTRICT, WARD, COUNCIL_TAX, BROADBAND, and MEDIAN_HOUSE_PRICE. Connected tables through unique identifiers such as Ward_Code.

SQL and R Integration:Employed RSQLite and DBI packages for seamless interaction between R and SQLite databases. Executed various SQL queries to extract meaningful insights, such as average house prices, broadband speeds, and council tax charges.
